Nusa Penida

Nusa Penida is a must – visit island, just ten minutes away by ferry from the main island of Bali! The island is known for its amazing views of Kelingking Beach, Angel’s Billabong and Broken Beach.

After getting off the ferry, the sites are an hour and a half drive. Be ready for steep roads, going uphill and downhill. My family and I first went to Angel’s Billabong and Broken Beach. Angel's Billabong is a stunning natural tide pool nestled between jagged rocks and cliffs. Just a short walk from Angel's Billabong is Broken Beach, another breathtaking sight. This natural marvel features a large natural archway. The natural bridge, formed by centuries of erosion, offers panoramic views of the surrounding coastline and ocean beyond.

Just thirty minutes away from Broken Beach is Kelingking Beach. The highlight of Kelingking Beach is its iconic cliff formation, which resembles the head of a Tyrannosaurus Rex dinosaur when viewed from above. Visitors are allowed to trek down to the beach. However, the trail is quite steep and rugged, thus requiring a lot of caution. My family and I chose to forgo the trek.

All these sites are popular tourist destinations on Nusa Penida, drawing visitors from around the world with their unique beauty and awe-inspiring landscapes. Whether you're admiring the view from above or exploring the shoreline up close, these natural wonders are sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone lucky enough to visit.

    Travel Tips:
    1. Be sure to visit these sites earlier in the morning rather than later in the day as they get quite crowded.
    2. Wear sunscreen and be sure to bring a cap and extra clothes as it can get very hot (up to 30 degrees Celsius during the day)
    3. Wear shoes as you may need to walk on rocks and other unstable ground
    4. Pack your own lunch as food options are limited on the island!
