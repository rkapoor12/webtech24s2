Lovina Beach

Against the breathtaking coastline of Bali, dolphin watching at Lovina Beach is an experience that gives tourists the chance to see wild dolphins in their natural habitat. Most recently, I had the opportunity to experience this in December of 2023.

Early in the morning is when most people go dolphin watching. We boarded a jukung, which are traditional outrigger boats. Skilled local fisherman who understand the behaviors and travel patterns of dolphins pilot these boats.

Me and my family left for Dolphin watching at around 6 am but spent an hour only looking for the dolphins. However, after an hour, we were able to spot dolphins.

The sight of these playful beings leaping and diving filled me with a sense of awe and wonder. They were so majestic but very quick. One moment they were on the surface and a second later, back underwater. They would then suddenly pop back up in a completely different area. I really wanted to also swim these dolphins.

Attached to these boats are wooden paddles that hang down and which you can hang on as the boat moves to snorkel. I did this and got to see the dolphins swimming underwater. That view was spectacular. Unfortunately, I did not have camera to capture this moment, however I highly recommend that you should carry one.
